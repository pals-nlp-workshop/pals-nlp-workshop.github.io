PERSONALIZATION (PALS)
Active and Passive LLM Personalization: When to personalize and what role do humans play in the personalization process?
EMNLP 2025 in Suzhou, China from November 5-9

Call for Papers

Large language models (LLMs) have demonstrated remarkable capabilities in various NLP tasks. However, the extent to which these models can and should adapt to individual users' needs remains an open question. Therefore, this workshop will focus on the personalization of LLMs to meet individual user's needs and preferences. From the user's perspective the personalization process can be either passive, where the LLM learns from observing user behaviour, or active, where the user directly guides the personalization. The topics of this workshop include any work related to active or passive LLM personalization, including but not limited to:
  • Learning and remembering user-specific information
  • Maintaining the context of prior conversations
  • Modeling the user's knowledge and expertise
  • Identifying and complying with preferred conversation styles
  • Mimicking the user's voice

Guidelines

We invite submissions of short (up to 4 pages) and long (up to 8 pages) papers, excluding references and the appendix. Submissions will be accepted via OpenReview, both as direct submissions and submissions via ARR. Please submit a single, anonymized PDF. The PDF should use the EMNLP LaTex template, following the conference formatting guidelines specified in the style files. We encourage you to include a limitations and ethics section in your paper. We will request at least 1 author per paper to participate in the reviewing process.

Papers will be non-archival and can be published on preprint servers, such as ArXiv.

The reviewing process will be double-blind. Please also note that reviewers will not be required to read the appendix.
